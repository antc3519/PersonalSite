3D Intensifies

A Collection

Since my Freshman year in high school I have been mesmerized by the creative powers of CAD programs and the capabilities they hold alongside 3D printers. In particular I have been very fond of a program called Blender which is very user friendly and had a large community behind it. As such I started creating random objects starting with a micro car modeled after an Anki Overdrive Racer.

This hobby later turned productive when I started creating Video Games with Unity and the amount of models I designed increased exponentially. Most of my models are fairly simple, I love geometric designs that show structure but not too much detail.

Scroll down to see a few interactive examples of my work. They are presented through sketchfab, check out my account there @anth3519.

Micro Car

This was my first 3d model and was mostly a learning experience as I tried to figured out how to properly use each button on the software. After a while I got the hang of it and this was my finished product! So why did I start off with a mini car? Starting up the program I asked myself, "What could I possibly make" and I had no clue until my eyes grazed over an Anki Overdrive Racer. (the car is like an electronic but slightly bigger version of a hot wheels car) That is where I found inspiration for my first model!
